# Mock Tests

Mock tests simulate the real exam: timed, scored, and reviewed.

## Setting up a mock test

1. Open **Mock Test** from the navbar.
2. Choose:
   - **Subject mix** — single subject or full mix.
   - **Number of questions** — typically 30, 60, or 120.
   - **Time limit** — auto-set based on question count, or custom.
3. Click **Start**.

## During the test

- A **timer** runs in the corner. The test auto-submits at zero.
- Use **Mark for review** to flag questions you want to revisit before submitting.
- A **question palette** shows answered, unanswered, and flagged questions at a glance.
- You can navigate freely between questions — there's no penalty for jumping around.

## After submitting

You'll get an instant report with:

- **Overall score** and percentile.
- **Per-subject breakdown** — strengths and weaknesses.
- **Question-by-question review** — every question, your answer, the correct answer, and the explanation.

> **Important:** mock tests are most useful when you treat them like the real thing. Find a quiet spot, stay off your phone, and resist the urge to peek at the explanations until you submit.

## Reviewing past attempts

All your mock tests are stored under your profile. Compare scores over time to see trends.
